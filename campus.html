Campus Cookbook

Was created with the intention of assisting college students who are navigating independent cooking for the first time, offering fast, nutritious, and straightforward meal suggestions. The application will showcase a range of recipes customized to match your dietary preferences.

Problem

According to our survey, 70% of students skip meals due to lack of time, directly affecting their health and academic performance."

Solution


"I designed the Campus Cookbook app to help students whip up meals in no time. It's got a cool fridge scanning feature and quick recipe tips, all wrapped up in a super user-friendly interface that actually makes cooking fun."

Research Insights

Limited Time

A significant 70% of students cited their busy academic and social schedules as the main reason for avoiding cooking, highlighting the need for quick and efficient meal solutions.

Fridge Scanning Feature

Innovation:

Introduced a fridge scanning feature to help students better utilize available ingredients and suggest recipes based on those ingredients.

Goal:

To reduce food waste and help students prepare a variety of dishes without the need for additional purchases.

Budget Constraints

Nearly 60% of the participants pointed out budget constraints as a key factor in their meal preparation decisions, emphasizing the need for cost-effective meal options that do not compromise on nutritional value

Limited Cooking Skills


Insight:

About 50% of students feel uncertain about their cooking abilities.

Application:

Alongside recipe suggestions, the fridge scanning feature can offer step-by-step cooking guides, boosting confidence among novice cooks by making the process straightforward and educational.

Affinity Diagrams

Key Findings:

Limited Time and Skills:

Students need fast, simple cooking options suited to their busy schedules and basic culinary skills.

Budget Constraints:

Financial limits require cost-effective recipes that still deliver on nutrition and flavor.

Cooking Methods:

The use of basic cooking appliances by students shows a need for recipes designed for minimal kitchen setups.

Design Variations

Iteration 1

The initial design used a raccoon mascot and bright, vibrant tones to create a fun and engaging app, appealing to users’ humor with the raccoon’s insatiable appetite, akin to hungry students.

Method:

Usability testing

Results:

  • Users had difficulty navigating the main menu.
  • Users thouth it is mobile version.
  • Users was dissapointed “why here is raccon”?

User Comments:

  • "I couldn't find the botton home easily."
  • There are too many details on the recipe cards. It feels a bit cluttered."
  • There are too many slides and surveys. It's tiring.

Iteration 2

The second iteration features a monochrome theme for a sleek, modern look, simplifying navigation and reducing visual clutter based on user feedback.

Method:

Usability testing with a new group of target users was conducted to gather feedback on the updated design and identify any usability issues.

Results:

  • Users suggested adding color highlights.
  • Some felt the landing screen needed more engaging elements.
User Comments:

  • The new design looks very clean and modern. It's much easier to navigate.
  • I love the monochrome theme, but it would be nice to have some color highlights.
  • The landing screen could use more visual appeal to make it more engaging.

Iteration 3

Method:

Usability testing was conducted to gather feedback on the updated design and identify any remaining usability issues.

Results:

  • Some users felt the new color scheme reminded them of a plant-related app.
  • Users found the instructions on how to use the app lacking.
  • Certain UI elements were not well-developed (e.g., excessive borders on the cards).

User Comments:

  • "The new green theme looks very fresh and natural."
  • "The green color scheme reminded me of a plant app."
  • "I didn’t find the instructions on how to use the app lacking."
