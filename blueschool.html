Blue Bear School of music

Blue Bear School of Music, founded in 1971 by the rock band "Wolfgang & Strauss" as “Blue Bear Waltzes School of Genuine Music,” is a nonprofit that partners with schools, shelters, and community centers to offer free music education to underserved populations.

Stakeholder Interview:


What are the primary goals of the Blue Bear School of Music website?


  1. Drive new students to sign up for lessons and classes
  2. Provide a portal for our existing students
  3. Increase Brand awareness and equity!
  4. Improve usability. Feedback that students are confused

Process Overview & The Problem


Problem

Users have a difficult time navigating process of finding a class to signing up.

Solution

Refine the website's navigation and site map for easier program access, and streamline the sign-up process to reduce clicks for class registration.
How might we improve the website’s listed services so that Blue Bear School of Music’s customers are successfully able to enroll?

Process

Responses

Key Findings

Offerings are unclear, Navigation is confusing

Developing solutions

I changed the location of class price and discounts information, and the data about discounts and offers. I also renamed the name of the groups and adjusted the discount process.

Wireframe

Then transitioned to wireframes to ensure our design addressed our original focus area of improved navigation.

Paper Prototypes / Usability Testing

I started by creating rough paper prototypes to collaborate on with team.

There were several design changes after discussions. A drop-down menu was added, and the way to get discounts or scale prices was updated. The homepage and navigation menu were redesigned. The stakeholder insisted on keeping the original color palette.

Conclusion

Blue Bear School of Music is a respected organization in the Bay Area community. The site already reflected their character, we just focused on improving navigation so more people would complete the signup process. Was helpful to get feedback from the one of their program directors on their focus areas to help define scope and avoid scope creep, especially with the constrained timeline.

FUTURE DEVELOPMENT

We’d recommend refining the ‘Community’ section of the site as many of the pages are outdated and difficult to navigate. We did not include this in our initial scope as the sign-up process was our main focus.
